Choosing to use a “Data Commons non-Distribution or Publishing license” [DC-NDP]

As with Creative Commons licenses, this Data Commons non-distribution suite of licenses ensures that you keep your copyright while allowing people to reliably and freely access, and re-use your data.

This ”Data Commons non-distribution suite of licenses” is akin to the Creative Commons suite of licenses, with the sole distinction that these non-distribution licenses explicitly debar the distribution, transmission, or publishing of the licensed dataset*.
That is: the end-user, in accessing and taking delivery of a dataset licensed as “non-Distribution”, agrees to a license which –while restricting them from publishing or re-distributing that dataset – DOES allows them to copy, use and refer to the dataset (as well as, if the license permits, to make derivative works from the data, which they may or may not be allowed to distribute)

  • Creative Commons (CC) licenses all permit “use, sharing, distribution and publishing” of the licensed item, and the “CC” icons are generally assumed and interpreted to mean that someone copying the licensed work has the right to distribute the licensed item.

  • The Data Commons non-distribution licenses, by comparison, allow an end-user to copy and use data, but not to distribute, re-distribute or publish the work.

Why would you wish to restrict “distribution by end-users”?
This “non-distribution and publishing” license is useful, where a data owner/licensor wishes to:

  • gather some use-metrics on their “shared” data; or

  • retain some version-control on their “shared” data
    (e.g provide a single-point-of-truth for their object, or provide a place to attach a point-of-truth version of the related metadata and other documentation and license; as well as a provide place to upload changes to their dataset, and to alert users to revision changes).

Additionally, there is not currently an easy and reliable way to bundle-up and keep connected a dataset, its license and its related metadata.
If a dataset is allowed to be, and then IS, freely distributed, the chance of a dataset being separated from its license, and then further redistributed and re-used without its license, is far increased. For government-generated data or similar – this may not matter greatly; However, for academic and other researchers producing data, where their professional effort and credibility is dependent on their research output (including their data), the above scenario of their data moving unlicensed into the public domain, is untenable.
Additionally, by using this non-distribution license in conjunction with an obligation for an end-user to register any derivative works onto the catalogue from which the data was downloaded, will provide an clear audit trail of the data’s re-use.



In these Data Commons non-distribution licenses, “re-use” means “copy for the individual end-user’s use, but NOT distribute or publish the licensed work”.

This licence allows the licensor to add further constraints on the re-use of their data, such as restrictions on:

  1. ATTRIBUTION: where “you must attribute the work used by you, and that attribution must be in the manner specified by the author, or licensor”.
    Eg you must give credit, and only on the conditions specified in the license or in the metadata record.

  2. NON-COMMERCIAL restriction: debarring the deriving of commercial gain as a result of use of the licensed data.

  3. DERIVATIVES restriction: creating derivatives from your data (e.g. modifying your work).

  4. SHARE_ALIKE restriction: having to apply “share-alike” to derivatives;


This licence also allows the licensor to add conditional exceptions to restrictions. (*thus, the licensor might add some condition which allows redistribution and publishing, contingent on permission having been granted by the licensor, or contingent on a specific time-period having elapsed, etc)



Below is a grid setting out some of the licensing combinations. The combination expected to be most often applied is the “DC-NDP-BY”, which simply requires attribution of the dataset, when used by an end-user. It is likely, and recommended that this licence also includes clarification on the types of derivative works allowed, and how these might be distributed, and attributed.
